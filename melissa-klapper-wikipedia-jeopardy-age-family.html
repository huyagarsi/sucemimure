Melissa Klapper Wikipedia; Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Melissa R. Klapper was born in the year 1973. Based on her birth year, Melissa is now 50 years of age. With American nationality, she has white ethnicity.

The Jeopardy! contestant comes from Merion Station, Pennsylvania. She eventually grew up in a family in Dallas. Later on, she moved to Baltimore with her parents when she was in the ninth grade.

Melissa grew up in a family where everyone loved knowledge-based quiz games. The family regularly played Jeopardy! which enticed her to try for a show as a teenager.

In fact, her parents drove her to Washington D.C. for trying out for Jeopardy! Unfortunately, she couldn’t make it into the show back then as she knew nothing about sports.

Melissa Klapper Education Details

Melissa earned her Bachelor of Arts degree from Goucher College in Towson, Maryland. Eventually, she completed her Ph.D. degree in history from Rutgers University.

The 50-year-old had been a bright student. Her scholarship has been awarded grants and fellowships from different institutes.

Some of these institutes include the Hadassah-Brandeis Institute, the Schlesinger Library on the History of Women at Harvard University, the National Endowment for the Humanities, the YIVO Institute for Jewish Research, and so on.

What Is Melissa Klapper Job?

As of now, Melissa serves as a professor of History and Director of Women’s & Gender Studies at Rowan University. She has been teaching American and women’s history at the university for more than 22 years.

Melissa mostly focuses on the late 19th and early 20th century. She aims at additional research interests in the history of American Jewish background, the history of education, and the history of childhood.

On top of that, Melissa is the coordinator of the women’s and gender studies program at Rowan University. She has also served as co-chair of Modern Jewish History.

Back in 2006, Melissa led the Memories of Migration series at the Haddon Township Branch of the Camden County Library. Moreover, she is also the author of several books.

Melissa Klapper As An Author; What Are Her Books?

Melissa has authored four books so far. She released her first book titled Jewish Girls Coming of Age in America, 1860-1920 in 2005.

Two years later, she released her second book titled Small Strangers: The Experiences of Immigrant Children in America, 1880-1925. Later in 2013, Melissa came up with her third book named Ballots, Babies, and Banners of Peace: American Jewish Women’s Activism, 1890-1940.
