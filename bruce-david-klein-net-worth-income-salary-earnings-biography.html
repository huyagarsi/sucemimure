Bruce David Klein Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Bruce David Klein is a prolific television producer, director, and writer who has worked in a variety of media, including cinema, television, and digital entertainment. Atlas Media Corporation was founded by him, and he now works as its president and executive producer. The History Channel, The Weather Channel, Factory Floor, Top 5 with Giada DeLaurentiis, and Behind the Bash with Giada DeLaurentiis are among the series for which Klein was responsible. On the television front, Klein was an early innovator in cable programming, responsible for series such as Dr. G: Medical Examiner (for Discovery Health/TLC), Breaking Vegas and History’s Lost & Found (for The History Channel), It Could Happen Tomorrow (The Weather Channel), It Could (Food Network). During his most recent tenure, he has been responsible for hundreds of hours of television, including “Big Sexy” on TLC, “Phowned!” on Spike TV, “Mary Knows Best” on SyFy, “Stalked: Someone’s Watching” on ID, “Alien Invasion: Are We Ready?” on Discovery Channel, “Big Brian: The Fortune Seller” on TruTV, and “Hotel Impossible” on TruTV (Travel). When it comes to theatrical documentaries, Klein directed Meat Loaf: In Search of Paradise (2008) and produced Robert Blecker Wants Me Dead (both in 2008). His other work includes the indie film The Stranger, which stars William Atherton and Roxana Zal and was written and directed by him. The TLC television series Who Killed Chandra Levy? was created and directed by Klein in 2011. Klein has moderated and participated on many panels for organisations such as the National Association of Television Program Executives, RealScreen, the Producers Guild, the USA Film Festival, and the New York University Film School. His work has appeared in and been praised in magazines such as The New York Times, Variety, Crain’s New York Business, Entertainment Weekly, TelevisionWeek, and World Screen News. He is a member of the American Society of Cinematographers. The National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences has inducted Klein as a member, and he presently serves on the Executive Committee of the National Association of Television Program Executives (NATPE).
