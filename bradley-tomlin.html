Top Facts about Bradley Walker Tomlin

  • Bradley Walker Tomlin was an American abstract expressionist painter.
  • He was born in 1899 and died in 1953 at age 53.
  • Tomlin studied under Arshile Gorky and John Graham.
  • His work is characterized by calligraphic lines and gestural brushstrokes.
  • Tomlin’s paintings are included in major museum collections worldwide.
  • He participated in the landmark exhibition “Abstract Painting and Sculpture” at MoMA in 1951.
  • Tomlin’s works often feature a balance between chaos and order.
  • He was influenced by Asian art, particularly Chinese calligraphy.
  • Tomlin’s painting “Number 16” sold for $12 million at auction in 2018.
  • The artist suffered from alcoholism, which contributed to his early death.
