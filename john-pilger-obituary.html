John Pilger obituary

John Pilger, the esteemed Australian journalist, writer, and documentary filmmaker, passed away on December 30, 2023, at age 84.

He is survived by his partner Yvonne Roberts, and two children.

Pilger’s life was marked by a relentless pursuit of truth, an unyielding commitment to justice, and a fearless dedication to challenging the status quo.

Born in Sydney in 1939, Pilger’s career spanned decades and left an indelible mark on the world of journalism. He rose to prominence as a fierce critic of Western foreign policy, courageously exposing the human toll of government interventions and corporate agendas.

His scrutiny extended to conflicts such as Vietnam, Cambodia during the Khmer Rouge era, and the Iraq War, earning him respect for providing alternative perspectives, even in the face of controversy.

Pilger’s legacy as a documentary powerhouse is reflected in over 50 films that tackled critical global issues, from nuclear disarmament to Indigenous Australian rights and the Palestine-Israel conflict.

His groundbreaking 1979 documentary, “Year Zero: The Silent Death of Cambodia,” remains a testament to his ability to shed light on hidden atrocities and bring about international awareness.

Throughout his illustrious career, Pilger received numerous accolades, including two Journalist of the Year Awards in Britain, recognition for his documentaries, and a prestigious visiting professorship at Cornell University.

Beyond the awards, John Pilger’s true legacy lies in his unwavering dedication to truth and justice.

He was a voice for the voiceless, challenging powerful narratives and inspiring generations of independent journalists to follow suit.

While his critical views were not universally embraced, his impact on journalism and documentary filmmaking is undeniable.

John Pilger leaves behind a legacy of courage, integrity, and a reminder of the profound importance of independent journalism in shaping a more just and informed world.
