Jonathan Isaac Bio

Jonathan Isaac’s romance with this handball game started during his high school days. He first performed at Barron Collier High School in Naples, Florida. This was during his freshman and sophomore years. He left Barron for the International School of Broward located in Hollywood, Florida. He had his college and high school basketball career in Broward but would later go on to study at IMG Academy. Isaac accomplished a lot as a high school basketball player. Among other things, the record states that he was able to average 17.6 points and 10 rebounds while leading the AMG Academy 21-10.

Beyond his skills and talent, the seed of growth Throughout his high school career, he did a lot to set him apart from other players. This allowed him to become a combo forward, a position he thrived at. Given his ability on the field, it was no surprise that he was given five-star rookie status, in addition to being ranked the 12th best player in the Class of 2016 by ESPN.

Despite confirming that he was going to commit to After Florida State High School, on February 5, 2016, he announced his intention to enter the 2016 NBA Draft, the first American postdoc to go straight from high school in the NBA draft since 2005. However, just four days after the announcement, Isaac changed his mind and reaffirmed his commitment to play for Florida State for the 2016-17 season.

Jonathan spent a year in Florida before announcing his intention to forfeit his final three years of college eligibility and enter the 2017 NBA Draft. On June 22, 2017, he was selected with the sixth overall pick by the Orlando Magic.

Jonathan Isaac is one of six children born to his mother, Jackie Allen. Isaac is the fourth child, with two older brothers, an older sister and two younger brothers in his family. Basketball is of Puerto Rican descent on his mother’s side and can play for the Puerto Rico national team. This highly sought-after team has been courted by the Puerto Rican team and has expressed an interest in playing for the Puerto Rican team if his friend Maurice Harkless of the Portland Trailblazers asks him to play for the team.

Career statistics

Jonathan Isaac has racked up impressive numbers as a basketball player. Starting in high school, Jonathan averaged 17 points per game and 10 rebounds. It was an impressive record in his college career. In the second game of his freshman season in college, he recorded 20 points. He later recorded his first double of the season with 14 points and 10 rebounds, earning him the CAC Freshman Player Award for his performance. On several occasions during the season, Jonathan Isaac scored over 20 points in a game, so much so that he was considered a team leader alongside potential teammates and teammates Dwayne Bacon and Xavier Rathan-Mayes.

Unfortunately, during his first and only season at the college level, Jonathan did not win any tournaments. He scored just 25 points in the two games he played in the NCAA Tournament. At the end of the season, he was named to the 2017 All-ACC Freshman Team.

Jonathan was unable to replicate his best school and college performances in the NBA due to injuries. He missed 55 games in his rookie year and sprained his right ankle within 10 minutes in Magic’s first preseason game of the 2018-19 NBA season. His NBA career tally is currently 27 games played, he started 10 averaging 19.9 minutes per game. He’s averaging 5.4 points and 3.7 rebounds per game.
